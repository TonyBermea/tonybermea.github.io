Web App · 2025

A data-dense comparison dashboard for a specialty-coffee roaster: it scores and compares green-coffee batches across weighted criteria in one unified view. The domain is specialty coffee — the engineering is bid leveling.

The brief

Give the roaster a single screen to evaluate multiple coffee candidates across many scored attributes, surfacing quality outliers and pricing anomalies at a glance — the same core UI problem behind scope comparison and vendor qualification.

Approach

A scrollable CSS-Grid matrix with sticky headers maps 10 scored attributes across 5 candidates. An interpolated color engine conditionally formats every cell — weak scores in red, exceptional ones in green, cost anomalies on a separate purple scale. A one-click "level to baseline" toggle recomputes each cell as a signed deviation from its row average, with animated transitions and a diverging palette. A shared hover state cross-links the matrix to a price-vs-quality scatter and a morphing radar profile, so hovering one candidate lights up all three panels at once.

Outcome

A production-grade, linked decision-support tool — hand-built SVG with no charting dependency, a decoupled color-scale and data layer, full light/dark theming with no-flash init, and reduced-motion support. Built with React 18, Vite and Motion; 92 KB gzipped.

eCommerce · 2024

A streetwear eCommerce storefront with a bold-but-minimal aesthetic — oversized type and full-bleed product imagery carried by generous negative space.

The brief

A direct-to-consumer streetwear label needed a shop that felt as considered as the drops themselves: high-impact and distinctive, yet clean, fast and effortless to browse.

Approach

A responsive product grid with a strong typographic hierarchy and a restrained, near-monochrome palette lets the garments lead. Collection filtering, quick-add, and a slide-in cart keep the path to checkout short, while optimized imagery and lazy loading keep it quick on mobile.

Outcome

A storefront where the design steps back and the product steps forward — a bold first impression with a frictionless route to purchase.

Editorial · 2024

A concept for the landing page of an online magazine, built around a dynamic, editorial layout — a modular grid that reflows around the day's featured stories rather than a fixed template.

The brief

Explore how a magazine homepage could feel alive and print-like: confident hierarchy, expressive typography, and a composition that adapts to whatever the lead stories are.

Approach

An asymmetric, magazine-style grid pairs large display type with a serif reading face, while scroll-reveal and subtle hover motion give the page rhythm. It reflows cleanly from multi-column spreads on desktop to a single, readable column on mobile.

Outcome

A flexible editorial layout that bends to the content instead of the other way around — visually striking without sacrificing readability.

NFT Platform · 2023

The interface for an NFT platform — a gallery-first product where the artwork leads and the on-chain machinery stays quietly in the background.

The brief

Make minting, browsing and collecting feel approachable: a marketplace that reads like a gallery rather than a trading terminal.

Approach

A visual-first collection grid and focused artwork detail views put the work center-stage. Wallet-connect, mint and bid flows are reduced to a few clear steps, with live status — price, edition, owner — surfaced without clutter, all on a dark, high-contrast theme that lets the art pop.

Outcome

A clean, confidence-building marketplace UI that foregrounds the art and makes the web3 steps feel routine.
